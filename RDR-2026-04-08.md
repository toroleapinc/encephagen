## 5. 后天 Phase: Distributed Learning

### 7 Learning Rules Implemented

| Organ | Rule | What it does |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Cerebellum | Supervised LTD | Climbing fiber error → weaken granule→Purkinje |
| Basal ganglia | Dopamine RL | RPE → strengthen D1 (reward) or D2 (punishment) |
| Hippocampus | Hebbian LTP | Co-active CA3 neurons → strengthen recurrent |
| Amygdala | Pavlovian | Threat + sensory → strengthen LA→CeA |
| Brainstem | Habituation | Repeated stimulus → synaptic depression |
| CPG | Sensory adaptation | Proprioceptive feedback → adjust tonic drive |
| Cortex | Homeostatic | Very slow firing rate adaptation |

### 4 Months of Development Results

| Month | Survival | vs Innate |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Innate | 251 | 1.0x |
| Month 1 | 244 | 0.97x |
| Month 2 | 241 | 0.96x |
| Month 3 | 238 | 0.95x |
| Month 4 | 251 | 1.00x |

Motor control: flat. But internal changes are real:

| Organ | Weight Change | What Happened |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| **Amygdala** | **+50.9%** | Learned fear of falling (LA→CeA strengthened) |
| **Thalamus** | **-11.7%** | Habituated to constant sensory input |
| **Superior colliculus** | +4.6% | Visual pathways slightly strengthened |
| **Basal ganglia** | +3.6% | D1/D2 drives adjusted from reward |
| **Hippocampus** | +2.4% | CA3 recurrent connections strengthened (memory traces) |
| **Cerebellum** | +1.0% | Granule→Purkinje adjusted from motor errors |
| **Cortex** | 0.0% | No change (homeostatic too slow) |
| **Spinal CPG** | 0.0% | No change |

State changes: arousal spiked (0.42→7.66), dopamine dropped (0.30→0.18), serotonin depleted (0.30→0.00), hunger accumulated (0.31→0.51).

### Interpretation

The brain IS learning — in the right places (amygdala learns fear, hippocampus forms memories, thalamus habituates). But motor control doesn't improve because:
1. Cortex hasn't changed (0%) — needs cortical takeover (corticospinal myelination)
2. The brainstem reflexes still dominate motor output
3. Cerebellum learning is too slow to affect body control significantly

This matches real infant development: the first months show internal brain reorganization (state changes, fear learning, habituation) before visible motor improvements appear around month 3-4.
